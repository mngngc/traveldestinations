My Check-ins

Wat Phra

Reclining Buddha Temple at Bangkok

We took a tuktuk (Thailand traditional taxi) to come visit the infamous reclining buddha statue which is one of the largest statue in Thailand. The statue was absolutely magnificent with the height of 15m and the length of 46m (I didn't include a picture because frankly, there's no way to fully capture the whole statue). It took us around 2 hours just to walk around the temple.

China Town

A hug of Chinese and Thai culture

We came to Chinatown for streetfood and hidden bars as one of my friends recommendation. When we first arrive, we were all overwhelmed by the lights, the smell of stirfried food, the noises of the vendors and the visitors. I can't remember what exactly did we get for dinner but I remember an amazing dessert made with tapioca and coconut milk. Such a nice way to explore the Bangkok nightlife!

Koh Phi Phi

The island with "The Beach"

I became known of Phi Phi island through the movie The Beach (2000) starring Leonardo DiCarpio. This island was under visiting-restriction by the Thailand government until recently due to the devastating impact of mass tourism on the ecosystem. We were so lucky to be one of the firsts to be back on this beautiful island. It was exactly like in the movie, with emerald-colored water, white sand beach, and friendly people.
